Macaulay's Coefficients

Given $d\in\mathbb Z_{\geq 0}$, each $a\in\mathbb Z_{\geq 0}$ has a unique representation as a sum of binomial coefficients

$a=\binom{b_d}{d}+\binom{b_{d-1}}{d-1}+\cdots+\binom{b_j}{j}$,

where $b_d>b_{d-1}>\cdots>b_j\geq j\geq 1$.

$a^{\langle d \rangle}=\binom{b_d+1}{d+1}+\binom{b_{d-1}+1}{d}+\cdots+\binom{b_j+1}{j+1}$,

where $0^{\langle d \rangle} = 0$.
